## 5. What Information the App Processes

For clarity, "processes" includes any action the App performs with information, including reading it from the device, displaying it, storing it locally, or transmitting it to a third party.

### 5.1 Information You Provide Directly

You may, at your option, provide the following information to the App. None of this information is required to use the core training and nutrition features.

- **Profile information:** name, date of birth, biological sex, height, body weight, training experience level, sport, daily activity level, preferred unit system, and preferred one-rep-max formula.
- **Training and nutrition logs:** workout sessions, sets, repetitions, weights, custom exercises, custom recipes, meal entries, food preferences, allergens, disliked ingredients, pantry items, hydration entries, workout templates, session feedback (RPE, soreness, sleep), and free-text notes.
- **Subscription tier preference:** which paid tier (if any) you elect to subscribe to through Apple's StoreKit framework.
- **Preference toggles:** biometric lock on/off, profile-tab visibility, optional analytics opt-in.

### 5.2 Information Processed Through Apple Sign In

If you choose to use Sign in with Apple (this is **optional**), Apple provides the App with a stable, opaque user identifier (and, if you so consent at the Apple system prompt, a name and email address). The opaque identifier is stored only in the iOS Keychain on your device. We do not transmit it to any server because **we do not operate one**.

### 5.3 Information Processed Through Apple HealthKit

If you grant permission, the App may read from and write to your device's Apple HealthKit store under the categories listed in our HealthKit usage strings, which currently include: body mass, height, biological sex, date of birth, active and resting energy, heart rate (instantaneous, resting, and zone summaries), workout sessions and routes, dietary energy and macronutrients, and dietary water (hydration).

**HealthKit data never leaves your device through the App.** Apple HealthKit data is stored by Apple in an encrypted local database on your device and (if you have enabled it in iOS settings) synced via end-to-end-encrypted iCloud. The App reads HealthKit data only into memory for display and writes its own logged workouts and meals back to HealthKit so they appear in the Health app. We do not exfiltrate HealthKit data to any third party, and we do not use HealthKit data for advertising, marketing, or to sell or trade with any third party. This conforms to Apple's HealthKit Data Use Policy and to App Store Review Guideline 5.1.3.

### 5.4 Information Processed Through Apple Watch

If you use the Apple Watch companion, the watchOS app records workout sessions through `HKLiveWorkoutBuilder`, samples heart rate, and (with permission) records GPS-based workout routes through CoreLocation. Logged sets and metrics sync to the iPhone over the Apple-provided `WCSession` connection. None of this data is transmitted off your devices through the App.

### 5.5 Information Processed Through the OpenFoodFacts API

When you scan a barcode or perform a manual food search, the App sends only the barcode digits or the text of your search query to `world.openfoodfacts.org`, the public OpenFoodFacts open-data project. We do not send any identifier that ties the request to you, and we do not log that request server-side because **we do not operate a server**. OpenFoodFacts may log requests according to its own privacy policy (see https://world.openfoodfacts.org). The App receives food data in response and stores any meals you choose to save in your local SwiftData database.

### 5.6 Information Processed Through Apple's In-App Purchase System

All subscription purchases are processed by Apple through StoreKit 2. The App receives only an entitlement signal indicating whether a tier is active. We do not see or receive your payment card information, Apple ID, billing address, or any other personal information related to the transaction. Refer to Apple's privacy policy at https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/ for details.

### 5.7 Information Processed Through EventKit (Reminders)

If you choose to export a shopping list to Apple Reminders, the App writes reminder entries to your local Reminders database with your permission (`NSRemindersFullAccessUsageDescription`). The App does not transmit those entries anywhere; they live in your Apple Reminders account subject to Apple's privacy practices.

### 5.8 Information Processed for Authentication

If you enable the optional biometric lock, the App invokes Apple's `LocalAuthentication` framework. **The App never sees, stores, or transmits your fingerprint, Face ID data, or device passcode.** Authentication is performed entirely by the operating system; the App only receives a success-or-failure result.

### 5.9 Information Processed for Local Telemetry

The App maintains a local SwiftData log of usage events (such as app launches, routines started, meals logged, paywall views, score views, food scans) in a `UsageEvent` table. **These events are stored only on your device.** As of the Effective Date, no transmission of telemetry to any remote server is configured or enabled. If we ever activate remote telemetry, we will (a) update this Privacy Policy and present a notice in-app before transmission begins, (b) update the App Store privacy questionnaire, and (c) make transmission contingent on your express opt-in via the existing "Help improve Building Routines" toggle in Profile, which defaults to OFF.

### 5.10 Information Automatically Processed by Apple

Apple's own analytics (App Analytics, crash reports submitted via the Apple system "Share with App Developers" toggle) and the App Store may transmit aggregate, anonymized information to Apple and, in the case of opt-in crash reports, to us in anonymized form. This processing is governed by Apple's privacy policy. We do not receive any individually identifiable information from Apple unless you specifically initiate it (for example, by emailing us a support request).

### 5.11 Information We Do **Not** Collect

We do not collect, and the App does not transmit to any server we operate:

- Your name, email, or phone number (we have no contact list);
- Your precise or approximate location, except as recorded into your local HealthKit store for workout routes you start on Apple Watch;
- Your photos, microphone audio, contacts, calendars (except Reminders entries you create), or text-message content;
- Your IP address, advertising identifier (IDFA), or any cross-app or cross-site tracking identifier;
- Any payment card or financial information;
- Any government identifier (Social Security Number, driver's license number, etc.);
- Any biometric identifier or biometric template within the meaning of New York General Business Law § 899-aa(1)(b)(ii).

## 6. How We Use Information

All processing of information by the App is performed **on your device** for the following purposes:

- to display your training history, nutrition history, and progress;
- to compute training recommendations, AI-composed plans, load suggestions, warm-ups, effectiveness scores, and nutrition targets through deterministic Swift functions running locally on your device;
- to write your logged workouts and meals to Apple HealthKit so they appear in Apple's Activity and Nutrition surfaces;
- to authenticate you locally via Apple Sign In and (if enabled) biometric lock;
- to validate your active subscription entitlement via Apple StoreKit;
- to look up food data through the OpenFoodFacts public API based on a barcode you scan or a search term you type;
- to export shopping lists to Apple Reminders if you so direct;
- if and only if you have explicitly opted in, to create a local telemetry record that may, in the future, be transmitted in aggregated form to help us improve the App.

We do not use your information for behavioral advertising, profiling, automated decision-making with legal effect, or sale to third parties.

## 9. Data Retention & Deletion

Because all personal information described in Section 5 lives on your device, **you control retention by controlling your device**:

- **In-app deletion:** Profile, training, nutrition, recipe, plan, and pantry data can be deleted individually from within the App.
- **Health data:** Data written to Apple HealthKit by the App can be deleted from the iOS Health app at any time. Revoking the App's HealthKit access in iOS Settings does not delete data already written.
- **Watch data:** Data on the Apple Watch is removed when the watch app is uninstalled or when the Watch is unpaired and reset.
- **Subscription data:** Subscription history is held by Apple and can be reviewed and managed through your Apple ID account.
- **App deletion:** Deleting the App from your device permanently removes all local SwiftData stores, the local telemetry log, and Keychain entries created by the App. Apple Health data and Reminders entries you previously created are not deleted by uninstalling the App; manage those in the Health and Reminders apps respectively.

If you correspond with us by email, we retain that correspondence for as long as is reasonably necessary to address your inquiry and to comply with applicable record-keeping obligations, after which we delete it.

## 10. Security

We implement and maintain reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect the limited personal information the App processes against unauthorized access, destruction, use, modification, or disclosure, as required by the SHIELD Act, N.Y. Gen. Bus. Law § 899-bb. These safeguards include, without limitation:

- storing the optional Apple Sign In identifier exclusively in the iOS Keychain;
- making no calls to any first-party server we control;
- using HTTPS / TLS for the single outbound API call (OpenFoodFacts);
- conducting periodic review of dependencies and code for security defects.

No method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure. To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, we make no warranty that our safeguards will prevent every possible compromise.
