Building Routines — Support
Welcome! This page covers the most common questions about Building Routines for iPhone and Apple Watch. If you don't find your answer here, contact options are at the bottom.

Getting started
Install Building Routines from the App Store (requires iOS 18 or later).
On first launch you can sign in with Apple or skip sign-in entirely — no account is required to use the app.
Open Profile (the avatar in the top-left of any tab) and set your sport, experience level, and body details. The more you fill in, the better your routines and nutrition targets get.
Optional: connect Apple Health from Profile → Integrations so finished workouts count toward your Activity rings.
FAQ
General
What is Building Routines? A strength-training and nutrition tracker that ties your workouts, food, and goal together: guided and custom routines for 14 sports, a daily AI workout plan, an exercise library with step-by-step how-tos, meal logging with barcode scanning, a macro-aware meal planner, and an Apple Watch companion for live workouts.

What devices are supported? iPhone running iOS 18 or later. The Apple Watch companion requires watchOS 10 or later. iPad and Mac versions are planned for a future update.

Do I need an account? No. Everything works without signing in. Sign in with Apple is optional and only used to remember your identity for features like Biometric Lock.

Subscriptions & billing
What's free and what's paid? The core app is free: routines, the daily AI workout plan, the exercise library, custom exercises, meal logging, the recipe library, short-range meal planning, shopping lists, trends, sharing, and Apple Health integration. Two optional subscription modules add more:

Tier	Monthly	Annual
Analytics Pro	$2.99	$19.99
Nutrition Pro	$4.99	$34.99
Complete (both + all future paid modules)	$6.99	$49.99
Complete includes a 7-day free trial. Prices are in USD; your local price is shown in the app before you buy.

How do I cancel or change my subscription? Subscriptions are billed and managed by Apple. Open Settings → your name → Subscriptions on your iPhone (or App Store → your profile → Subscriptions), select Building Routines, and change or cancel there. Canceling keeps your access until the end of the paid period.

I subscribed but the features didn't unlock. Open the paywall (for example from the Nutrition tab) and tap Restore Purchases. If you use multiple Apple IDs, make sure you're signed into the one that purchased.

Can I get a refund? Refunds are handled by Apple, not by us. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com and sign in with the Apple ID used for the purchase.

Apple Watch
How do I install the Watch app? After installing on iPhone, open the Watch app → My Watch tab → scroll to Available Apps → tap Install next to Building Routines. (If "Automatic App Install" is on, it installs by itself.) Requires watchOS 10 or later.

How do workouts on the Watch work? The Watch is built for recording, not data entry. Tap Start Workout to begin a live session with heart-rate streaming, log quick sets with the Digital Crown as you train, and tap End when you're done. Back on your iPhone, the workout appears under Routines → Watch workouts, where you name it and assign your sets to real exercises — at the gym you just record; the details come later.

The first time I tapped Start Workout it asked for Health access. Why? Live heart rate and workout recording use Apple's HealthKit. The Watch asks once; if you decline, you can re-enable access later in the Watch's Settings → Privacy & Security → Health.

I logged sets on the Watch but they're not on my phone. Sets sync over the wireless connection between Watch and iPhone. If your phone is out of range or the app isn't reachable, sets are queued on the Watch (you'll see a "queued" count on its start screen) and sent automatically the next time the two connect. Nothing is lost.

Apple Health
What does the app read and write? With your permission it reads weight, height, age, sex, and heart-rate samples to personalize training, and writes finished workouts and logged meals so they show up in the Health app and your Activity rings. You can change permissions anytime in Health app → your profile → Apps → Building Routines, or on the Watch under Privacy & Security → Health.

Does my health data leave my device? No. Health data is read from and written to Apple's Health store on your devices. We operate no servers and never receive it.

Food & nutrition
How does barcode scanning work? Scanning a food barcode looks the product up in Open Food Facts, a public food database. Only the barcode digits (or your typed search text) are sent — never your identity, location, or health data. Camera images are processed on-device and are not stored or transmitted.

How does the meal planner work? The planner builds meal plans that fit your calorie and macro targets, budget, and season. Free users can plan up to 3 days at a time; Nutrition Pro extends that to 30 days with goal-aware targets and adherence tracking. Plans generate a grouped shopping list you can check off in-app, export to Apple Reminders, or share.

Privacy & data
Where is my data stored? On your device. Workouts, meals, body data, recipes, and plans live in local storage; nothing is uploaded because there is nothing to upload it to — the app has no backend. Details: Privacy Policy.

How do I delete my account or data? If you signed in with Apple, open Profile → Delete Account and confirm. Because the app has no backend, this permanently removes your Apple sign-in from the device Keychain and erases all Building Routines data on the device — profile, workouts, meals, recipes, plans, and pantry — returning the app to its first-launch state. You can also delete individual records within the app, or delete the app entirely. Sign out (Profile) is different: it removes only your Apple sign-in and keeps your local data. If you granted Health access, anything written to Apple Health stays there under Apple Health's control and can be deleted from the Health app.

Does the app track me or show ads? No ads, no trackers, no third-party analytics SDKs. An optional, off-by-default usage-analytics toggle exists in Profile; even when enabled, events currently stay on your device.

Troubleshooting
The app asks for camera / Face ID / Reminders access — why? Camera: barcode scanning only. Face ID: the optional Biometric Lock feature. Reminders: only when you export a shopping list to Apple Reminders. Each is requested the first time you use the feature and never before.

Something else is wrong. Force-quit and reopen the app first. If the problem persists, grab your version number from Profile → About (there's a copy button) and include it when you contact us — it helps a lot.
